Classes by Year

Available Classes

Transfiguration (1st Year)

Transfiguration is the art of changing the form or appearance of an object. In first year, students start with simple transformations like turning a matchstick into a needle.

History of Magic (2nd Year)

A theoretical subject that covers significant magical events and figures throughout history. Second-year students focus on the medieval period and the formation of the Ministry of Magic.

Defense Against the Dark Arts (3rd Year)

This course teaches students how to defend themselves against dark creatures, curses, and hexes. In third year, students begin learning about more dangerous magical creatures and defensive spells.

Herbology (4th Year)

Herbology is the study of magical plants and their properties. In fourth year, students learn about dangerous and highly magical plants like Mandrakes and Venomous Tentacula.

Potions (5th Year)

Potions is the study of brewing magical mixtures. By fifth year, students tackle complex potions like the Draught of Peace and Veritaserum, requiring precision and patience.
